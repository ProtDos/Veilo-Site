What is Veilo?

Veilo is a private messaging app that protects your metadata, encrypts your communications, and makes sure your messaging activities leave no digital trail behind.

How does Veilo work?

Veilo uses advanced encryption algorithms, proven to be quantum proof. We are implementing a great list of mechanics to hide any of your activity from third parties - and us, in case we can a cease of desist letter. Learn more.

Can you read my messages?

No! Not even us can read any of your messages, as they are end-to-end encrypted. We don't even know who you are, when and to whom you are writing. There is absolutely no meta-data.

If my phone is taken from me, can someone access my messages?

Veilo allows users to encrypt their local Veilo database with a PIN code. With this feature turned on, your messages cannot be accessed without knowing your PIN code.

How does Veilo protect my identity?

You don’t need a mobile number or an email to make an account with Veilo. Your display name can be your real name, an alias, or anything else you like. Veilo does not collect any geolocation data, metadata, or any other data about the device or network you are using. At launch, Veilo used proxy routing to ensure nobody can see who you’re messaging or the contents of those messages. Shortly after launch, Veilo moved to our onion routing system, which we call onion requests, for additional privacy protection.

Is my IP address being revealed when I use Veilo?

In messaging apps, metadata is the information created when you send a message — everything about the message besides the actual contents of the message itself. This can include information like your IP address, the IP addresses of your contacts, who your messages are sent to, and the time and date that messages are sent. It’s impossible for Veilo to track users’ IP addresses because the app uses onion requests to send messages. Because Veilo doesn’t use central servers to route messages from person to person, we don’t know when you send messages, or who you send them to. Veilo lets you send messages — not metadata.
